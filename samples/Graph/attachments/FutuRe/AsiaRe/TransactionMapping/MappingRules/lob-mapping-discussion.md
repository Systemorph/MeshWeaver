Hi Wei,

As you know, we're onboarding AsiaRe into the group profitability analysis platform. The Singapore office asked me to propose how our local Japanese/APAC lines of business should map to the group standard classification.

The challenge is that our local product structure is quite different from what EuropeRe or AmericasIns use. Our main lines are:

- **Earthquake & Volcanic** — This is our largest line. Japan's seismic exposure drives most of our nat-cat book. It covers commercial and residential earthquake, volcanic eruption, and tsunami.
- **Typhoon & Flood** — Seasonal weather catastrophe covering the typhoon belt (Japan, Philippines, Taiwan, Vietnam) plus monsoon flooding across South and Southeast Asia.
- **Paddy & Crop** — Agricultural reinsurance focused on rice paddy, tea, and tropical fruit crops. Very specific to Asian agriculture — monsoon-dependent with government-backed schemes in several markets.
- **Motor Third-Party** — Compulsory motor third-party liability across APAC. Regulatory frameworks differ significantly between Japan, India, Thailand, and Australia.
- **Construction & Engineering** — Large infrastructure projects: high-speed rail, offshore wind, semiconductor fabrication plants, port development. Asia's construction boom makes this a major line for us.
- **Trade Credit & Surety** — Import/export credit insurance and surety bonds supporting intra-Asian trade corridors (China-Japan, ASEAN, Australia).
- **Micro Health & Personal Accident** — Micro-insurance health and PA products distributed through mobile platforms in Southeast Asia (Indonesia, Philippines, Vietnam). Very high volume, very low premium per policy.
- **Cyber & Digital** — Technology liability and cyber insurance for Asian tech hubs (Tokyo, Singapore, Shenzhen, Bangalore). Growing fast but still small relative to our nat-cat book.

None of these map cleanly to a single group line. For example, our Earthquake line is mostly Property at the group level, but the volcanic/tsunami specialty component probably belongs under Specialty. Our Construction line spans Property, Energy, and Professional Liability.

Could you advise on how to structure the percentage splits? I'd like to present a proposal to the APAC actuarial committee next month.

Best regards,
Yuki

Hi Yuki,

Thanks for the detailed overview. You're right that the Asian product structure is quite distinct — this is exactly the kind of situation the transaction mapping framework was designed for.

Here's my suggested mapping based on our experience with EuropeRe and AmericasIns:

| Local LoB | Group LoB | Suggested Split | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|-----------|
| Earthquake & Volcanic | Property | 80% | Core earthquake coverage is property risk |
| Earthquake & Volcanic | Specialty | 20% | Volcanic/tsunami is specialty peril |
| Typhoon & Flood | Property | 70% | Weather damage to property |
| Typhoon & Flood | Marine | 30% | Coastal/port flooding, cargo damage |
| Paddy & Crop | Agriculture | 90% | Primary agricultural risk |
| Paddy & Crop | Property | 10% | Farm structure/infrastructure damage |
| Motor TPL | Casualty | 100% | Standard motor liability |
| Construction & Engineering | Property | 60% | Physical damage to structures |
| Construction & Engineering | Energy | 20% | Power/infrastructure components |
| Construction & Engineering | Professional Liability | 20% | Design/engineering PI |
| Trade Credit & Surety | Casualty | 50% | Credit default is casualty-type risk |
| Trade Credit & Surety | Marine | 30% | Trade/cargo nexus |
| Trade Credit & Surety | Professional Liability | 20% | Surety professional element |
| Micro Health & PA | Life & Health | 80% | Health and personal accident |
| Micro Health & PA | Casualty | 20% | PA liability component |
| Cyber & Digital | Cyber | 70% | Core cyber risk |
| Cyber & Digital | Professional Liability | 30% | Tech E&O component |

These splits ensure each local LoB sums to 100% and the group-level view gives a meaningful picture of AsiaRe's contribution to the consolidated P&L.

I'd recommend presenting this to the actuarial committee with a note that the splits should be reviewed annually as the portfolio mix evolves — particularly the Cyber line, which is growing rapidly.

Let me know if you need any adjustments before the committee meeting.
